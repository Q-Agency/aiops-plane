# Agency OS — Vision Deep-Dive & Upgrade Plan

**Status:** review artifact #3, companion to `docs/product-vision.md` and `docs/product-vision-screens.md` · **Date:** 2026-06-09 · **Inputs:** eight-lens adversarial review (consistency, cutting-edge, ai-native, strategy, ux-quality, governance, operating-model, contract) + blind-spot pass, judged and deduped. Convergence notes and citations preserved. All locked product decisions respected — nothing below relitigates the seven-role catalog, dedicated-per-client, mock-first, or the three pillars.

## Verdict

The plan's bones are genuinely strong, and specifically strong: the FIRE UP / RUN / MONITOR spine, the honesty discipline (Live/Roadmap badges, "when-only" actor on the ledger), the deterministic-vs-LLM wall on `/governance`, the §8 deferred-decision register, and a screens doc that embeds its own coverage review and a reconciled mock-data appendix — most funded products ship with less. The three differentiated assets (accountability matrix, zero-LLM validators, per-delivery-unit economics) are real and rare.

It is weakest exactly where it was edited last and where it touches money, humans, and regulation: the UI/UX role was inserted at the pitch layer and never propagated (the hero demo chain is forked across five places); the ROI wow number cannot survive a competent CFO; the humans are modeled as infinitely available while the plan engineers approval fatigue; the product never talks, learns, or proposes; pricing was set by accident in mock copy; and a product wearing an EU-West badge has no EU AI Act story eight weeks before Article 26 applies. Three silent architectural reversals (in-app approval, agent configuration, pod scoping) are unregistered against the plan's own "nothing dropped silently" rule.

**Bottom line:** the experience plan is fundable and the moat is real, but the demo as scripted cannot be performed, the money math self-destructs at contract time, and the policy layer behind the governance evidence doesn't exist — fix those before a single component is mocked.

## P1 — build into the plan now

### Governance & policy

**P1-G1. Gate policies and graduated autonomy** *(converged: governance, operating-model, ai-native — five findings merged; the most converged strategic gap in the review).* The entire approval model is hardcoded convention: no approval matrix (who approves which artifact class, N-eyes, delegation), no risk tiering, no batch review or sampling, no autonomy dial — while the mandatory typed reason on **every approve** guarantees "Looks good" ×40 and a ledger full of worthless attestations. The mock's own data shows a saturated human (Ivan: 9 decisions/day × 38 min ≈ 5.7 h/day). **Upgrade:** one `src/mock/gatePolicies.ts` layer — `GatePolicy { artifactKind; requiredRole[]; nEyes; slaClearMin; delegation; overridePolicy }` — seeded from blueprint defaults; risk-tiered modes (full / batch / auto-with-sampling); free-text required only on **reject** and **override** (structured quick-reason chips for clean approvals); policy chips on gate rows and the Spec Review header. Layer the **autonomy ladder** on top: L0 review-all → L1 batch → L2 sample 1-in-5 → L3 auto-low-risk, promotion criteria = deterministic validator streaks ("BA eligible for L2: 47 consecutive 8/8 specs, 4% rejection"), proposed by the system, **granted by the accountable human, written to the ledger**. Frame in vision §1 as the moat's second act: *deterministic checks are the precondition machine for safe delegation*. Lands in: screens (Gates, Spec Review, Settings or wizard step 5b), vision §4/§5/§8.

**P1-G2. The Slack approver sentence guts attribution** *(converged: governance, operating-model).* "Approvals posted here can be **actioned by anyone in the channel**" (screens line 337) contradicts required-reason, one-accountable-human, role capability `approve`, and the decision audit — the cheapest approval path bypasses every control MONITOR just sold, read aloud *during the FIRE UP beat*. **Upgrade:** approvals announced in-channel become **deep-link-only** (opening My Gates with sign-in + role + reason), routed to the gate's accountable human/delegate, with an audited "act on behalf of Ana (recorded as override)" path; clarifications stay anyone-can-answer, with the asymmetry stated in copy. Flag that BA's real `slack_approver_ids` behavior must converge before any compliance claim is sold. Lands in: screens (Wire Slack), agent-sdk-brief note.

**P1-G3. ### Operating model

One §8 row for the runtime mechanism; surfaces and policy are mock-now. ## Blind spots

Dimensions every lens missed because all eight reviewed from *inside the dashboard frame*. Two are P0 and covered fully above: **#1 Brownfield coexistence** (→ P0-7) and **#2 The model plane** (→ P0-8).

**3. The client's engineers and their repo (P1).** GitHub is wired as a connector, yet the docs contain zero occurrences of "pull request" or "branch." Unspecced: the Dev agent's git identity (bot account, signed commits, `Co-authored-by:` the accountable human), the PR contract (spec link + gate id + validator summary in every PR description), branch-protection/CODEOWNERS interplay, and the duplicate-review collision — engineers review code *in GitHub*, the product specs a code-review gate *in `/approvals`*. **Add:** a "Code-side integration model" subsection in vision §5 with the explicit **gate↔PR mirroring rule**: the code-review gate *is* the GitHub PR review (webhook resolves the gate; dashboard shows status + deep-links out) — the deliberate inverse of the spec gate, justified per persona; a FIRE UP readiness check ("Bot collaborator invited · branch-protection compatible"); `src/mock/codeIntegration.ts` with two seeded PRs. *Speculative extension:* repo-native governance receipts — a verifiable governance block in every agent PR, so the client's repo carries the audit trail even if they drop Agency OS.

**4. Deliverables have no shelf (P1).** Artifacts exist only at gate-time; there is no library of what the pod *produced* — and the architecture's own audit-DB rationale records that **BA's reset deletes the spec**. "Where's the spec we approved last month?" currently answers "possibly deleted." **Add:** **Deliverables — `/artifacts`** (MONITOR): filterable artifact table (kind, ticket, version, approved-by joined from the ledger), per-artifact version timeline with rejected-iteration diffs (reuse `buildLineage` + `MarkdownLite`), "Export all" — plus the load-bearing rule in §8: *approved artifacts are snapshotted into the control plane at gate-clearance*.

**5. No story for Agency OS itself failing (P1).** Agents keep working when the dashboard is down (an accidental, never-stated resilience property), in-app gates become unreachable (Slack is the de facto fallback, never designed as one), and the audit sync stops while the agent bus is ephemeral — **permanent, silent ledger gaps** in a record sold as gap-free; no platform SLA, status page, or DR statement. **Add:** a §8 "Platform availability & DR" row (uptime target, ledger RPO, correlated model-provider-outage stance); a designed degraded-mode banner in the App Shell ("agents continue working; gates remain answerable in Slack; the ledger backfills on reconnect") with **gap-marker rows** in Compliance ("sync gap 02:10–02:34" — the when-only honesty pattern again); a per-tenant `/status` page (component health, 90-day uptime) doubling as a procurement artifact.

**6. No adversarial-input threat model (P1).** The pod ingests untrusted third-party text by design (customer-submitted tickets, Slack, Drive, email-roadmap) and holds write scopes — the textbook injection-exfiltration trifecta — yet neither doc mentions prompt injection, and a reviewer approving a subtly-shaped spec has no provenance cues. **Add:** a §8 "Adversarial-input threat model" row (SDK-side filters/canaries, per-connector write allowlists); an **input-provenance chip** on Spec Review ("Sources: 1 customer-submitted ticket · 2 internal docs — external content treated as untrusted"); a `suspicious-input` incident kind, seeded once; one vision §4 line framing validators + gates + provenance as the *layered injection defense*.

**7. The agents speak English; the buyers don't all (P1).** §8 scopes i18n to UI formatting, but the **work language** is uncovered — and the 8 structural validators parse English EARS keywords and SPEC.md section structure: *the moat is currently monolingual*, in Q's home EU market. **Add:** `language` on `PodBlueprint`/`PodDraft` with one FIRE UP select ("Working language — specs, reports, and gates are produced in this language"); a §8 row with a stated stance (EARS keywords stay English as a structural convention with localized body text — defensible and demo-sayable — or validator locale packs), decided before the first non-English pilot; tooltip translations on EARS chips in Spec Review.

**8. No exit story (P2).** An anti-lock-in pitch with no answer to "what happens when we leave": artifact/IP ownership, generated-code licensing warranty, ledger custody after the contract ends (the client's retention obligations outlive the relationship, yet the ledger lives in Q's Supabase), decommission attestation — the entire legal surface is `dpaSigned: boolean`. **Add:** a §8 "Exit & IP" row (work-for-hire assignment, offboarding runbook incl. ledger handover); extend `ExportDataDialog` into the one-click **"Offboarding pack"**; add *Exit* to vision §10's pushback list.
